Learning Plan

19 September 2019

What is your long term goal and or career pathway?

I have a position lined up to start at the beginning of February 2020, and it is my goal to be job-ready at this time. After this, it is actually my intention to not have a long-term career plan. I have been on a long-term career progression pathway up until this time, and would like to take the time to forge my own path. In my new role next year, I will be rotating through four different departments at my new company, and I would like to choose my onward career path based on my experience in these different technology fields.

What do you think your biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp?

I am a driven and fast learner who loves solving problems, which will help me along my learning journey as I pick up new concepts and technologies. I am also highly adaptable and used to a constantly changing environment, which I believe will also aid me during Bootcamp.

I do find that I have a strong need to prove myself and to excel in whatever I do, across all aspects of my life. While this drives me to succeed, it can also lead to significant stress and burnout, and to fluctuations in self-esteem when things don't pan out quite as intended. Having experienced burnout in my previous career, I am aware that this is something I am susceptible to - and I am setting goals around prioritising self-care because of this.

What skills would you like to see developed in yourself while at EDA?

I would like to grow my skills in developing projects as a member of a team. Most of my experience to date has been in the creation and development of my own personal projects, so this is something that I have had limited exposure to. As part of this I would like to be more comfortable when working with other people's code - a crucial skill as I move into a professional capacity working with large codebases. I would also like to prepare myself as much as possible for dealing with imposter syndrome, which I believe I will experience as I enter the workforce - especially entering a grad program in which the majority of my cohort will have completed Computer Science or Software Engineering degrees.

What are your expectations from the Bootcamp team?

I expect to be stretched throughout my time in Bootcamp, and also to learn in a supportive and friendly environment.

What are your expectations of yourself on Bootcamp?

I feel that my challenge during this programme will be in finding balance between my studies and my personal life. I am aware that I am prone to burnout, so focusing on self-care will be a top priority for me as I progress through this course. I am hopeful that having some job security will allow me to study in a healthier way - enjoying the challenge rather than becoming stressed by it.

I am a dance teacher/business owner, and will need to have Tuesday evenings away from the course/my computer in order to run classes. I will also need a few weekends off - for a combination of dance events and a wedding. I will ensure that I work more hours around these times if required to complete my course work.
