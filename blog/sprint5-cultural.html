Core Reflections

20 September 2019

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

I was interested to learn that self-regulation is considered to be a component of emotional intelligence; I had previously thought of this as a separate entity.

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

Having to write and share these blog posts has been the most challenging part. I personally don't find writing to be the most helpful reflection medium; I prefer to internalise my reflections and then talk things through with close friends and family. I also felt uncomfortable with having to share these posts online. If I am going to perform a reflective deep dive and uncover new things about myself, I am not going to be sharing these new personal findings online with strangers.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development bootcamp course?

I think that core skills are incredibly important in the workforce. Employers want to hire people who are personable and good at working in teams. Having empathy and compassion for others will go a long way in helping teams to work more effectively with less friction, with a resultant increase in workplace mood and productivity.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practicing programming instead? Justify your answer.

I do feel that there were definitely times that core learning felt like a waste of time; it was certainly the part of each sprint that I wanted to do the least. I am a reasonably introspective/reflective person - and while some of the learnings have been interesting, I don't really feel like it has changed my view of myself or the way in which I think.
